WEST BENGAL PREMISES TENANCY ACT, 1997
(Rent Control — West Bengal)

Source: legislative.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS FOR TENANTS:

1. SECURITY DEPOSIT LIMITS
Under the West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act 1997, a landlord cannot demand a security deposit exceeding three months' rent. Any amount collected beyond this is recoverable by the tenant.

2. RENT INCREASE RESTRICTIONS
A landlord cannot increase rent arbitrarily. Any rent increase must follow the procedure laid down under the Act. The tenant has the right to challenge unreasonable rent hikes before the Rent Controller.

3. EVICTION PROTECTIONS
A tenant cannot be evicted without a court order. The landlord must file a suit before the Rent Controller and prove valid grounds for eviction such as:
- Non-payment of rent for two consecutive months
- Subletting without written consent
- Causing damage to the property
- Landlord's bona fide personal need

4. ESSENTIAL SERVICES
The landlord is prohibited from cutting off essential services (water, electricity) to force a tenant to vacate. This is a punishable offence under the Act.

5. RECEIPT OF RENT
The tenant has the right to demand a written receipt for every rent payment. If the landlord refuses to accept rent, the tenant can deposit it with the Rent Controller.

6. REPAIRS AND MAINTENANCE
The landlord is responsible for structural repairs. The tenant is responsible for day-to-day maintenance unless otherwise agreed in writing.

7. HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT
- Approach the Rent Controller of your district
- File a written complaint with details of the dispute
- No lawyer is required — you can represent yourself
- The Rent Controller must dispose of the case within a reasonable time
