RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005 (RTI)

Source: india.gov.in, rti.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS FOR CITIZENS:

1. YOUR RIGHT TO KNOW
Every citizen of India has the right to request information from any public authority (government office). The public authority must respond within 30 days. This is a fundamental right.

2. WHAT YOU CAN ASK FOR
- Copies of any government document
- Details of government decisions and policies
- Information about government spending and contracts
- Status of any government application or complaint
- Inspection of government works and records

3. HOW TO FILE AN RTI APPLICATION
Step 1: Write a simple application addressed to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the relevant government department.
Step 2: State clearly what information you need. You do NOT need to give a reason.
Step 3: Pay the application fee of ₹10 (by cash, demand draft, or postal order). BPL (Below Poverty Line) card holders are exempt from fees.
Step 4: Submit in person, by post, or online at rtionline.gov.in.

4. ONLINE RTI FILING
- Visit: rtionline.gov.in
- Select the department
- Fill in your details and question
- Pay ₹10 online
- Track your application status online

5. TIMELINE
- Response must come within 30 days
- If it concerns life or liberty: within 48 hours
- If PIO transfers to another department: 35 days total

6. FIRST APPEAL
If you don't receive a response or are unsatisfied:
- File a First Appeal with the First Appellate Authority (senior officer in the same department)
- Must be filed within 30 days of the response (or non-response)
- No fee for first appeal

7. SECOND APPEAL
If the First Appeal fails:
- File a Second Appeal with the Central/State Information Commission
- The Commission can impose penalties on officers who deny information without valid reason

8. EXEMPTIONS
RTI does not cover:
- Information affecting national security
- Cabinet papers
- Personal information with no public interest
- Information protected by court orders

9. PENALTIES
If a PIO fails to provide information without reasonable cause:
- Penalty of ₹250 per day of delay
- Maximum penalty: ₹25,000

RTI FORM A (SAMPLE):
To: The Public Information Officer
[Name of Department/Office]
Subject: Application under RTI Act, 2005
Sir/Madam,
I request the following information under the Right to Information Act, 2005:
[Your specific question]
I am paying the prescribed fee of ₹10.
Name: ___
Address: ___
Date: ___
