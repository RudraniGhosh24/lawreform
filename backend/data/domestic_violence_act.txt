PROTECTION OF WOMEN FROM DOMESTIC VIOLENCE ACT, 2005

Source: legislative.gov.in, india.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS:

1. WHAT IS DOMESTIC VIOLENCE
The Act defines domestic violence broadly to include:
- Physical abuse: hitting, slapping, kicking, or any physical harm
- Sexual abuse: forced sexual intercourse or any sexual act against will
- Verbal and emotional abuse: insults, ridicule, threats, humiliation
- Economic abuse: not providing money for necessities, controlling finances, preventing employment

2. WHO IS PROTECTED
- Any woman in a domestic relationship: wife, live-in partner, mother, sister, daughter
- The relationship can be by blood, marriage, adoption, or living together like a family

3. WHO CAN BE COMPLAINED AGAINST
- Husband or male live-in partner
- Relatives of the husband (mother-in-law, father-in-law, etc.)
- Any adult male member of the household

4. YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THIS ACT
a) Right to Residence: You cannot be thrown out of the shared household. Even if the house is not in your name, you have the right to live there.
b) Protection Orders: The court can order the abuser to stop violence, not contact you, stay away from your workplace/home.
c) Monetary Relief: The court can order the abuser to pay for medical expenses, loss of earnings, maintenance, and damages.
d) Custody Orders: Temporary custody of children can be granted to the woman.
e) Compensation: The court can order compensation for injuries and emotional distress.

5. HOW TO GET HELP
Step 1: Contact a Protection Officer (available in every district)
Step 2: Call Women Helpline: 181 (toll-free, 24/7)
Step 3: Call Police: 100 or visit the nearest police station
Step 4: File a Domestic Incident Report (DIR) with the Protection Officer
Step 5: The Protection Officer will help you file a case in the Magistrate's Court

6. IMPORTANT POINTS
- Filing a complaint is FREE
- You do NOT need a lawyer (the Protection Officer assists you)
- The Magistrate must hear the case within 3 days of receiving the application
- The court can pass an ex-parte order (without hearing the other side) in urgent cases
- Violation of a protection order is a criminal offence (imprisonment up to 1 year)

7. FREE LEGAL AID
If you cannot afford a lawyer, you are entitled to free legal aid under the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987. Contact your District Legal Services Authority (DLSA).

8. SHELTER HOMES
If you need a safe place to stay, the Protection Officer can arrange accommodation in a shelter home.

EMERGENCY CONTACTS:
