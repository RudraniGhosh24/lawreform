CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019

Source: legislative.gov.in, india.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS FOR CONSUMERS:

1. YOUR RIGHTS AS A CONSUMER
- Right to Safety: Protection against goods and services that are hazardous to life
- Right to Information: Right to know the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, and price of goods
- Right to Choose: Access to a variety of goods at competitive prices
- Right to be Heard: Right to file complaints and be heard at consumer forums
- Right to Redressal: Right to fair settlement of genuine grievances
- Right to Consumer Education: Right to knowledge about consumer rights

2. WHAT YOU CAN COMPLAIN ABOUT
- Defective goods (broken, expired, not as described)
- Deficient services (poor quality, delayed, not as promised)
- Unfair trade practices (false advertising, misleading claims)
- Overcharging (charging more than MRP)
- Restrictive trade practices

3. WHERE TO FILE A COMPLAINT
Based on the value of goods/services:
- Up to ₹1 crore: District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum
- ₹1 crore to ₹10 crore: State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission
- Above ₹10 crore: National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission

4. HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT
Step 1: Write a complaint letter with:
  - Your name and address
  - Name and address of the seller/service provider
  - Description of the problem
  - Relief you are seeking (refund, replacement, compensation)
  - Supporting documents (bills, receipts, photos)
Step 2: Submit to the appropriate Consumer Forum
Step 3: Pay a nominal fee (₹100 to ₹5,000 depending on claim value)

5. ONLINE COMPLAINT
- File online at: consumerhelpline.gov.in
- National Consumer Helpline: 1800-11-4000 (toll-free)
- E-daakhil portal: edaakhil.nic.in (file cases online)

6. TIMELINE
- The Forum must resolve the complaint within 3 months (if no testing required)
- Within 5 months (if testing of goods is required)

7. PRODUCT LIABILITY
Manufacturers, sellers, and service providers can be held liable for any harm caused by defective products or deficient services. You can claim compensation for:
- Physical injury
- Property damage
- Mental agony

8. E-COMMERCE PROTECTIONS
The Act covers online purchases too:
- E-commerce platforms must display seller details
- Easy return and refund policies must be provided
- Grievance officer details must be displayed on the website

9. PENALTIES
- Misleading advertisements: Fine up to ₹10 lakh (₹50 lakh for repeat offence)
- Adulterated/spurious goods: Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to ₹5 lakh
