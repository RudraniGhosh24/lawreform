DOMESTIC WORKERS' RIGHTS IN INDIA

Source: india.gov.in, labour.gov.in (Public Domain)

CURRENT LEGAL FRAMEWORK:

Note: India does not yet have a comprehensive central law specifically for domestic workers. However, several existing laws provide protections:

1. CODE ON WAGES, 2019
- Domestic workers are covered under the Code on Wages
- They are entitled to minimum wages as fixed by the state government
- Several states have notified minimum wages for domestic workers
- Employers must pay wages on time and cannot make unauthorized deductions

2. SEXUAL HARASSMENT OF WOMEN AT WORKPLACE ACT, 2013
- Domestic workers are covered under this Act
- A household is considered a "workplace"
- Domestic workers can file complaints with the Local Complaints Committee (LCC)

3. UNORGANISED WORKERS' SOCIAL SECURITY ACT, 2008
- Domestic workers fall under "unorganised workers"
- Entitled to social security schemes including:
  - Life and disability insurance (Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana)
  - Health insurance (Ayushman Bharat)
  - Old age pension (Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Maan-dhan)

4. STATE-LEVEL PROTECTIONS
Some states have specific rules for domestic workers:
- Kerala: Domestic Workers (Registration, Social Security and Welfare) Act, 2015
- Tamil Nadu: Manual Workers Act covers domestic workers
- Maharashtra: Minimum wages notified for domestic workers
- Karnataka: Domestic workers included in minimum wage schedule

5. RIGHTS YOU HAVE AS A DOMESTIC WORKER
- Right to minimum wage (check your state's notification)
- Right to weekly day off
- Right to be treated with dignity (no physical or verbal abuse)
- Right to safe working conditions
- Right to social security benefits
- Right against sexual harassment at workplace

6. WHAT TO DO IF YOUR RIGHTS ARE VIOLATED
- Contact the Labour Commissioner of your district
- Call the labour helpline: 14434
- For sexual harassment: Contact the Local Complaints Committee (through the District Officer)
- For physical abuse: File an FIR at the nearest police station
- For non-payment of wages: File a complaint with the Labour Commissioner

7. IMPORTANT SCHEMES FOR DOMESTIC WORKERS
- E-Shram portal (eshram.gov.in): Register as an unorganised worker to access benefits
- Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Maan-dhan: Pension scheme (₹3,000/month after age 60)
- Ayushman Bharat: Health insurance up to ₹5 lakh per family per year
