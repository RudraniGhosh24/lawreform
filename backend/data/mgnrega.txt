MAHATMA GANDHI NATIONAL RURAL EMPLOYMENT GUARANTEE ACT, 2005 (MGNREGA)

Source: nrega.nic.in, india.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS FOR RURAL WORKERS:

1. GUARANTEED EMPLOYMENT
Every rural household has the legal right to 100 days of wage employment per financial year. The government MUST provide this work. This is not charity — it is your legal right.

2. WHO IS ELIGIBLE
- Any adult member of a rural household
- Must be willing to do unskilled manual work
- Must apply for a Job Card at the Gram Panchayat

3. HOW TO GET A JOB CARD
Step 1: Apply at your Gram Panchayat office (free of cost)
Step 2: Provide a photograph and basic details
Step 3: The Job Card must be issued within 15 days
Step 4: The Job Card is free — no one can charge you for it

4. HOW TO DEMAND WORK
- Submit a written application to the Gram Panchayat demanding work
- You can demand work for any number of days (up to 100 per household per year)
- Work must be provided within 15 days of your application
- Work must be within 5 km of your village (if beyond 5 km, extra transport allowance is due)

5. UNEMPLOYMENT ALLOWANCE
If the government fails to provide work within 15 days:
- You are entitled to an unemployment allowance
- First 30 days: 1/4 of the wage rate
- After 30 days: 1/2 of the wage rate

6. WAGES
- Wages must be paid within 15 days of work completion
- Payment must be made directly to the worker's bank/post office account
- No contractor or middleman is allowed
- Current MGNREGA wage rates vary by state (approximately ₹220-350/day)

7. WORKSITE FACILITIES
Every MGNREGA worksite must have:
- Drinking water
- Shade for rest
- First aid kit
- Crèche (if 5+ children of workers are present)

8. COMPLAINTS AND GRIEVANCES
- Complain to the Gram Panchayat or Block Development Officer
- Call the MGNREGA helpline (state-specific numbers)
- File a complaint online at nrega.nic.in
- Social audit: Every MGNREGA work must be audited publicly — you have the right to attend

9. COMMON VIOLATIONS TO WATCH FOR
- Demand for money to issue Job Card (illegal)
- Payment through contractors (illegal)
- Delayed wage payment (violation — you can demand compensation)
- Denial of work without providing unemployment allowance (violation)
