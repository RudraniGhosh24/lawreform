LEGAL SERVICES AUTHORITIES ACT, 1987
(Free Legal Aid)

Source: nalsa.gov.in, india.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS:

1. YOUR RIGHT TO FREE LEGAL AID
Under Article 39A of the Constitution and the Legal Services Authorities Act 1987, the government must provide free legal aid to ensure that no citizen is denied justice because of poverty.

2. WHO IS ENTITLED TO FREE LEGAL AID
You are entitled to free legal aid if you belong to any of these categories:
- Member of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe
- Victim of trafficking or bonded labour
- Woman or child
- Person with disability
- Industrial worker earning less than the prescribed limit
- Person in custody (undertrial prisoner)
- Person with annual income below ₹3,00,000 (₹5,00,000 in Supreme Court cases)
- Victim of a mass disaster, ethnic violence, caste atrocity, flood, drought, earthquake, or industrial disaster

3. WHAT FREE LEGAL AID INCLUDES
- Free lawyer to represent you in court
- Payment of court fees and other charges
- Preparation of legal documents
- Legal advice and consultation
- Mediation and conciliation services (Lok Adalat)

4. HOW TO GET FREE LEGAL AID
Step 1: Visit your District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) office
Step 2: Fill out an application form (available free of cost)
Step 3: Provide proof of eligibility (income certificate, caste certificate, etc.)
Step 4: A lawyer will be assigned to you free of charge

5. WHERE TO FIND YOUR DLSA
- Every district in India has a DLSA
- Usually located in the district court complex
- Find your DLSA: nalsa.gov.in
- NALSA helpline: 15100 (toll-free)

6. LOK ADALAT (PEOPLE'S COURT)
- Free dispute resolution forum
- No court fee
- No appeal (decision is final and binding)
- Covers: motor accident claims, labour disputes, matrimonial disputes, utility bill disputes, bank recovery cases
- Held regularly in every district

7. LEGAL AID CLINICS
- Free legal advice clinics in villages, jails, and educational institutions
- Para-legal volunteers available in every Gram Panchayat
- Legal literacy camps conducted regularly

8. IMPORTANT POINTS
- Free legal aid is your RIGHT, not charity
- No one can deny you legal aid if you are eligible
- The quality of legal aid must be equal to private legal representation
- If your assigned lawyer is not performing well, you can request a change

CONTACT:
- NALSA (National Legal Services Authority): nalsa.gov.in
- Toll-free helpline: 15100
- Your District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) — located in the district court complex
