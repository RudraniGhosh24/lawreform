THE CODE ON WAGES, 2019
(Minimum Wage and Payment of Wages)

Source: legislative.gov.in (Public Domain)

KEY PROVISIONS FOR WORKERS:

1. RIGHT TO MINIMUM WAGE
Every employer must pay at least the minimum wage as fixed by the appropriate government. This applies to ALL workers — including daily-wage, contract, part-time, and domestic workers. No employer can pay below the floor wage set by the Central Government.

2. TIMELY PAYMENT
- Daily wage workers: Must be paid at the end of the same day or before the next working day
- Weekly wage workers: Must be paid before the last working day of the week
- Monthly wage workers: Must be paid before the 7th of the following month
- Fortnightly wage workers: Must be paid before the 2nd day after the end of the fortnight

3. NO UNAUTHORIZED DEDUCTIONS
An employer cannot deduct wages except for:
- Income tax
- Provident fund contributions
- Court-ordered deductions
- Absence from duty
- Damage to employer's property (with due process)
Total deductions cannot exceed 50% of wages.

4. EQUAL PAY FOR EQUAL WORK
No discrimination in wages on the basis of gender. Men and women performing the same work or work of similar nature must receive equal wages.

5. OVERTIME WAGES
If a worker works beyond normal working hours, they are entitled to overtime wages at twice the normal rate.

6. PENALTIES FOR EMPLOYERS
An employer who pays less than minimum wage faces:
- Fine up to ₹50,000 for first offence
- Fine up to ₹1,00,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 months for repeat offence

7. HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT
- Approach the Labour Commissioner of your district
- File a written complaint (no fee required)
- You can also call the national labour helpline: 14434
- Online complaint: shramsuvidha.gov.in

MINIMUM WAGE RATES (vary by state and skill level):
- Check your state's current rates at: labour.gov.in
- West Bengal minimum wage for unskilled workers: approximately ₹326/day (2024-25)
